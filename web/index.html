How to check your baby

  • Look at the whites of the eyes.
  • Check the gums.
  • Check the palms and soles.
  • Watch feeding and alertness closely.

Warning signs

  • Difficulty waking for feeds
  • Poor feeding
  • Floppiness or unusual drowsiness
  • Dark urine or pale stool

          Quick actions

          Postnatal follow-up checklist

          • Day 1: Check feeding, alertness, urine and stool.
          • Day 2-3: Check eyes, gums, palms and soles for yellowing.
          • Day 7: Review feeding, weight concern and persistent yellowing.
          • Day 14: Refer if jaundice is still present or worsening.

          What CHWs should prioritize

          • Jaundice in the first 24 hours.
          • Poor feeding with yellowing or unusual sleepiness.
          • Difficulty waking, floppiness, dark urine or pale stool.
          • Darker skin babies where yellowing may be harder to detect.
